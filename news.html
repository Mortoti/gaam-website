Government Begins Payment of Assembly Members

After years of dedicated service without financial compensation, assembly members in Ghana are finally receiving their well-deserved payments. Their work has been demanding, yet unpaid, making governance at the local level challenging.

President John Dramani Mahama, who pledged to address this issue, has now fulfilled his promise, ensuring that assembly members are recognized and supported for their crucial role in community development. This landmark development marks a turning point for local governance in Ghana.

GAAM President Commends President for Fulfilling His Promise

Hon. Frank Nelson Ackah, the President of GAAM, has expressed immense joy and gratitude to the President for fulfilling his promise to compensate assembly members. Speaking on behalf of his colleagues, he stated that assembly members across the country are excited about this development and are now more motivated than ever to give their best in service to their communities.

GAAM Launches Official Website

Under the tenure of Hon. Frank Nelson Ackah, GAAM has achieved a significant milestone with the launch of its official website. This platform aims to enhance communication, accessibility, and efficiency within the association, providing a central hub for news, updates, and essential resources. The website was designed and developed by Mortoti-Agogyi Jephthah Lorlornyo, the founder of NOLAP Technology.

Gladys Akotia Wins Prestigious Award for Gender Equality

Hon. Akotia Gladys, the Assemblywoman for Kefas Electoral Area, has been honored with an award for Gender Equality, Inclusion, and Women Empowerment during the International Women's Day Celebration in the Western North Region. This recognition highlights her relentless efforts in advocating for women's rights and ensuring inclusive governance in her community.
